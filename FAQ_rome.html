Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to some of the most common questions about visiting Rome.



What is the Roma Pass? How and where can I purchase it?

The Roma Pass is a three-day tourist card that entitles the holder to free or discounted access to some of Rome's top museums, as well as free use of the public transport system. You can purchase the Roma Pass online at www.romapass.it, at one of the Tourist Information Points upon arrival in Rome, or through the 060608 Contact Centre. When purchasing online, you'll need to select a pick-up location and date. After completing the details, you'll receive a confirmation code. Show your code and ID to one of our staff at the chosen pick-up location.



When are museums free in Rome? Do children pay for public transport?

To check when museums and archaeological sites are free, visit Musei in Comune Roma for municipal sites or Beniculturali for state-run sites. Public transport is free for children under 10 years of age. For more details, visit the ATAC official site.



How do I get accommodation?

For a comprehensive list of accommodations in Rome, visit www.060608.it. Navigate to the Hospitality macro-area and then the Sleeping section for detailed information.



Can my dog enter Museums/public transport?

Dogs are not allowed in museums, except for guide dogs. On public transport, small-sized animals (dogs, cats, birds) are allowed in the first and last carriages of regional rail lines if restrained in a carry cage or muzzled and on a leash. A full-price ticket is required for animals, while guide dogs travel free. A maximum of two dogs are allowed per bus.



Do I need permission to take photographs, videos or films in the City of Rome?

Yes, permission is required unless the photographs or videos are for personal use. Submit an application at least 20 days before shooting begins to the Authorisation Service for shooting films and taking photographs (Servizio Autorizzazioni Riprese Cinematografiche e Fotografiche di Roma Capitale). You can obtain the form at the Public Relations Office (Piazza Campitelli, 7, Tel: +39 06 67102964). To rent locations or shoot films/documentaries in Rome and the Lazio region, contact the Fondazione Roma Lazio Film Commission at romalaziofilmcommission.it.
